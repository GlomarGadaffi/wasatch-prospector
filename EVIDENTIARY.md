# evidentiary standing

This document is for the people who decide what a Mirkwood output is allowed to
mean: investigators, prosecutors, defense counsel, judges, oversight bodies, and
any analyst who hands a correlation to someone with the power to act on it.

[`DISCLAIMER.md`](DISCLAIMER.md) states the project's scope and the false-positive
problem in general terms. This document states the narrower, sharper thing: **what
this system's output is and is not as a matter of evidence, and exactly how
treating it as more than it is destroys cases instead of making them.**

It exists because the dangerous failure here is counter-intuitive. Everyone
assumes the risk of a cross-channel correlation tool is *false positives convict
the innocent.* That risk is real and is covered below. But the failure that puts
**guilty people back on the street** is the opposite one, and it is the reason
this document is written for stakeholders before an adversary writes it for them.

---

## 1. The one controlling fact

> **A correlation lock is a co-presence hypothesis, not an identification.**

Everything else in this document follows from that single sentence. A "lock" means
two pseudonyms — on two channels — shared a fingerprint or a place-and-time window.
It does **not** mean the system knows who, or even that the two emissions came from
the same device. It is a *lead*. It is the beginning of an investigation, never the
end of one, and never the proof.

The system now enforces this in software, not just in prose: every result the
analyst returns carries an `epistemic` classification (`CORRELATION_HYPOTHESIS` or
`OBSERVATION`), every classification is flagged `is_evidence: false`, and the CLI
prints that flag *above* the data so it cannot be screenshotted away. See §8 for
what is enforced today and what is still only written down.

---

## 2. What the output actually is

The analyst returns one of two kinds of result. Neither is an identification.

**OBSERVATION** — a single-channel read of raw emissions: pseudonyms, locations,
timestamps. This is a record of *what was received on the wire.* A BLE MAC, a SIP
extension, a P25 unit ID, a TMSI. A pseudonym is a label a protocol chose. It is
not a person, and the system has no map from one to the other.

**CORRELATION_HYPOTHESIS** — a cross-channel or spatiotemporal join: two pseudonyms
that share a `device_fingerprint`, or that fell inside the same location-and-time
window. This is the "lock." It is a *hypothesis of co-presence* generated by a
`JOIN`. The strength of that hypothesis is governed entirely by the base rate at
that place and time (§3), and the system does not — cannot — measure that base rate
from the data in front of it.

There is no third category. The system never emits "identity," "affiliation," or
"presence of a named individual," because it has no column that means any of those
things.

## 3. Base rates — why "high confidence" is the trap

The convergence claim in the README — that joint observation across *n* channels
drives the probability of identification toward 1 — is true **only as population
density falls.** It runs backwards in exactly the environments where these tools
get pointed: crowds, transit, hotels, protests, downtown cores.

The project's own worked number: a transit hub with **N = 500** devices, a 5-minute
window, and a per-pair coincidental co-location probability of 10⁻³ yields on the
order of **125 spurious locks per window.** Not 125 over a day. Per window.

This is the base-rate fallacy in operational form. If real shared-device events in
that hub are rare — say a handful per window — then the *overwhelming majority of
locks the system produces there are coincidence,* and no amount of "the math says
0.99" changes it, because the 0.99 was conditioned on an independence assumption the
crowd violates. A decision-maker who sees a confident-looking lock and a tidy map
pin is being shown the most misleading possible summary of a low-information event.

**The interface surfaces candidates. It does not adjudicate them. A high score is
not a high probability of identity; it is a high probability that two pseudonyms
were near each other, which in a crowd is unremarkable.**

## 4. Why it is inadmissible as identification — and the cascade

Here is the mechanism stakeholders most need to understand, because it is where the
guilty walk.

Suppose a Mirkwood-style correlation is treated as identification and enters a case
— as the basis for a stop, a warrant, or testimony. A competent defense does not
argue the suspect is innocent. It attacks **the method.** And the method hands them
the cross-examination:

- **Error rate (Daubert factor):** the project publishes ~125 false locks per
  window in a dense setting. An identification technique that cannot state an error
  rate below "most of its outputs in this environment are wrong" does not survive a
  reliability challenge.
- **Testability / falsifiability:** the published pipeline runs on **synthetic data
  only** and has **never been validated against ground truth.** There is no measured
  true-positive or false-positive rate from real deployments, because there are no
  sanctioned real deployments. There is nothing to validate against.
- **Peer review and general acceptance:** cross-channel pseudonymity collapse as an
  *identification* standard is not an accepted forensic method. It is a research
  framing.
- **Known operating limits:** the method's own documentation says it produces
  hypotheses, not identifications, and warns in writing against acting on a lock.

Put those in front of a judge and the technique is ruled unreliable. Now the
cascade:

1. The identification is excluded.
2. If the lock seeded a **warrant**, the warrant loses its predicate — and under
   *fruit of the poisonous tree*, the real, solid evidence found downstream of that
   warrant is **suppressed along with it.** The strong case dies because of the weak
   link that opened it.
3. Worse at scale: a single appellate ruling that the methodology is unreliable
   makes **every other conviction that leaned on it appealable at once.** Not one
   guilty defendant — a cohort.

This is the asymmetry: used as a *lead* that is independently corroborated, the
technique costs you nothing if it is later questioned, because the conviction stands
on the corroboration. Used as *proof,* it is a single point of failure that can take
the whole case — and adjacent cases — down with it. **The way to keep guilty people
convicted is to never let this output be the evidence.**

## 5. Forgeability — a lock can be manufactured

Co-presence is not just noisy; it is **forgeable.** Every channel Mirkwood ingests
carries a pseudonym that an adversary can emit:

- BLE/WiFi MACs can be set in software.
- SIP extensions and contacts are attacker-chosen strings.
- LoRa node IDs, P25 unit fields, and LTE temporary identifiers can be replayed or
  spoofed by anyone with the radio to transmit them.

Because the `device_fingerprint` is a deterministic hash of those pseudonyms
(`SHA-256` of a normalized MAC, an extension, a unit ID), **anyone who can emit the
inputs can manufacture a fingerprint** — and therefore manufacture a lock placing a
chosen identity at a chosen place and time, or place themselves somewhere they were
not to pre-poison a future case.

The schema today has **no provenance and no integrity attestation.** An observed
emission and an injected one are indistinguishable in the database. The consequence
for evidence is total: the moment a defense demonstrates in one case that a lock is
forgeable — and it is trivially forgeable — *every lock the system has ever produced
inherits that reasonable doubt,* including the true ones. Forgeability is not a
per-case weakness; it is a class-wide one.

## 6. The lane this project endorses — and its preconditions

The disclaimer already names the only application the project supports:
**after-the-fact pattern establishment through lawful process** — developing, from
lawfully collected or lawfully released data, a pattern of the form "this location
exhibited this emissions profile across this date range," to inform a FOIA request,
a discovery filing, a civil-rights complaint, or an investigative brief.

For any output to even be *discussed* as evidence in that lane — none of which is
satisfied by the published, synthetic-only system — at minimum all of the following
would have to hold, and be documented:

- **Lawful acquisition** of every input, with standing to use it.
- **Provenance** for every emission: which sensor, which firmware, which clock,
  attested at capture — so an injected packet can be distinguished from an observed
  one (it cannot be today).
- **Time discipline:** a disciplined, logged clock source; spatiotemporal joins are
  meaningless if timestamps are not trustworthy to the window width.
- **Integrity:** capture-time hashing and a chain of custody from sensor to query.
- **Reproducibility:** the exact query, schema, and thresholds preserved, so the
  result can be independently re-run and challenged — the analyst already returns the
  generated SQL for exactly this reason.
- **Analyst blindness:** the correlation generated by rule, not reverse-engineered to
  fit a suspect already in mind.
- **Stated error rate** measured against ground truth for the specific environment.

Until those hold, the honest label is the one the software now prints:
**investigative lead, not evidence of identity.**

## 7. Bright lines — do not cross

For anyone in a position to act on an output, the following are out of bounds, and
restate the disclaimer's prohibited uses in evidentiary terms:

- **Do not** present a correlation lock as an identification, in an affidavit, a
  warrant application, testimony, or a charging decision.
- **Do not** use a lock as the sole predicate for a stop, search, seizure, or any
  real-time intervention. In a dense environment the lock is more likely coincidence
  than not (§3).
- **Do not** treat a `device_fingerprint` match as proof the same person, or even the
  same device, was present — the inputs are forgeable (§5).
- **Do not** strip, summarize away, or override the `is_evidence: false`
  classification when passing a result to a decision-maker.
- **Do** use a lock only to *direct* lawful, independently-corroborated
  investigation, and let the corroboration — never the lock — be the evidence.

## 8. What is enforced vs. what is still prose

Honest accounting of where the structural guarantees actually are:

**Enforced in software today:**
- Every analyst result is tagged with an `epistemic` classification
  (`CORRELATION_HYPOTHESIS` / `OBSERVATION`), `is_evidence: false`, and a pointer to
  this document. (`adapters/analyst.py: classify_epistemic_status`)
- The CLI renders that classification as a banner *above* the rows.
  (`adapters/analyst_cli.py: print_epistemic_banner`)
- The generated SQL is returned with every result for audit and reproduction.
- Queries are read-only at the SQLite engine level.

**Still only prose (this document and the disclaimer):**
- The base-rate / inadmissibility / forgeability analysis above. A reader can ignore
  a document; they cannot yet be stopped by the schema.

**Roadmap — the next increment that would move guarantees from prose into the data
model:**
- Per-row **provenance and integrity** fields on `EmissionEvent` (sensor identity,
  capture-time attestation, hash) so observed and injected emissions are
  distinguishable.
- A **calibrated confidence** on every correlation that carries the local density
  prior, so a lock cannot be displayed without the base rate that conditions it.
- A **non-strippable** evidentiary classification stored with the record rather than
  attached at the interface.

Until those land, the rule for every stakeholder is the sentence this document
opens with: **a correlation lock is a co-presence hypothesis, not an
identification.** Build the case on what corroborates it. Never on the lock.
